Tom Holland
Peter Parker
A 15-year-old gains spider-like abilities from a radioactive spider. Tom Holland was cast as Spider-Man after impressing producers with his roles in The Impossible, Wolf Hall, and In the Heart of the Sea. He aimed to bring a fresh take to the character, balancing teenage issues with superhero duties. To prepare, Holland attended the Bronx High School of Science, where his anonymity mirrored Parker's.
Zendaya
MJ
Zendaya was already a big star when she was cast as MJ in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, starting with 2017's “Spider-Man: Homecoming.” By that point in her career she had already amassed a huge following as the star of two Disney Channel hits, “Shake It Up” and “K.C. Undercover,” plus popped up in Beyonce's “Lemonade”
Robert Downey Jr.
Tony Stark
In "Spider-Man: Homecoming," Robert Downey Jr. plays Tony Stark/Iron Man, who mentors Peter Parker. Stark provides Parker with a high-tech Spider-Man suit and guidance on being a hero. Although he appears in a limited capacity, Stark's influence is significant, shaping Parker's development and decisions throughout the film.
Jacob Batalon
Ned Leeds
In "Spider-Man: Homecoming," Jacob Batalon plays Ned Leeds, Peter Parker's best friend. Ned is a loyal and enthusiastic sidekick who discovers Peter's secret identity as Spider-Man early in the film. He provides comic relief, tech support, and moral support to Peter, often helping him with various superhero tasks and dilemmas. Ned's friendship and assistance are crucial to Peter's journey as both a high school student and a budding superhero.
Jon Favreau
Happy Hogan
Happy Hogan, the former head of security for Stark Industries and Tony Stark's driver and bodyguard, is looking after Peter Parker. Jon Favreau, who plays Hogan, finds it enjoyable to return as an actor, allowing him to maintain his relationship with the MCU. Favreau previously portrayed Hogan and directed the first two Iron Man films.
Michael Keaton
Adrian Toomes
Adrian Toomes becomes an arms trafficker using Chitauri tech after his salvage company fails. He's Liz's father, Peter Parker's love interest and a grounded villain, intended as a "regular guy" contrasting Spider-Man's "regular kid" persona. Director Jon Watts aimed for Toomes to avoid Avengers' attention and be a realistic foe for Peter Parker. Michael Keaton, playing Toomes, portrays him as a family-driven "dark Tony Stark."
Donald Glover
Aaron Davis
Aaron Davis, a criminal seeking to buy weapons from Toomes, is the uncle of Miles Morales, a future Spider-Man in the comics. Donald Glover, who voiced Morales in the Ultimate Spider-Man TV series and campaigned to play Spider-Man in 2010, was cast by director Jon Watts as a treat for fans. Davis mentions his nephew, hinting at Morales's potential future in the MCU. Watts insisted on Glover for the role, saying he would have removed Davis from the film if Glover declined.
Laura Harrier
Liz
Liz is a senior student with a "type-A" personality, Peter Parker's love interest, and Adrian Toomes' daughter.
Marisa Tomei
May Parker
Marisa Tomei plays Aunt May in the MCU, starting with "Captain America: Civil War" (2016). Her casting was controversial for being younger than previous versions. The creative team wanted a more naturalistic and closer-in-age portrayal. Tomei suggested Aunt May be a community organizer to reflect Peter's values.
Gwyneth Paltrow
Pepper Potts
In "Spider-Man: Homecoming," Gwyneth Paltrow plays Pepper Potts. She appears in a brief scene at the end of the movie where Tony Stark (Iron Man) proposes to her during a press conference. This cameo connects the film to the larger Marvel Cinematic Universe and continues the storyline of Tony and Pepper's relationship.
Tony Revolori
Flash
Tony Revolori plays Flash Thompson, Peter Parker's rival and classmate in the MCU. Unlike the traditional white bully from the comics, Flash is reimagined as a rich, smug social media bully. Revolori, a Guatemalan American actor, received death threats upon his casting but worked hard to honor the character. Flash's rivalry with Peter stems from jealousy over Peter's intelligence and popularity. Revolori gained 60 pounds for the role.
Bokeem Woodbine
Herman Schultz
An accomplice of Toomes' who is the second person to use modified, vibro-blast shooting versions of Brock Rumlow's gauntlets.
Jake Gyllenhaal
Quentin Beck
In "Spider-Man: Far From Home," Mysterio, a former Stark Industries employee and holographic-illusions specialist, poses as a superhero from Earth-833. Recruited by Nick Fury to help Spider-Man, he manipulates people's love of heroes. Actor Jake Gyllenhaal aimed to make Mysterio's fake backstory realistic, with director Jon Watts comparing Mysterio's relationship with Spider-Man to a "cool uncle," offering a surprising MCU introduction.

Roger Harrington

J. B. Smoove
Julius Dell
In "Spider-Man: Far From Home",J. B. Smoove reprises his role as Parker's teacher and a chaperone on his school trip to Europe. The role was written for Smoove after the writers and director enjoyed his performance alongside Holland in an Audi commercial short film produced to promote Homecoming
Samuel L. Jackson
Nick Fury
The former director of S.H.I.E.L.D., Nick Fury, finds himself without his usual control. Director Jon Watts describes Fury's relationship with Parker as "the mean new stepdad," in contrast to Tony Stark's "supportive cool uncle" role. Fury views Parker as a valuable asset distracted by high school issues. Initially, Watts pitched Fury as a mentor to Parker in Homecoming.
Cobie Smulders
Maria Hill
In "Spider-Man: Far From Home",Cobie Smulders reprises her role as a former high-ranking S.H.I.E.L.D. agent who works closely with Nick Fury.
Benedict Cumberbatch
Stephen Strange
In "Spider-Man: No Way Home," Benedict Cumberbatch reprises his role as Dr. Stephen Strange, acting more as a colleague than a mentor to Peter Parker. Cumberbatch sees a close bond between them, though their relationship deteriorates over the film. Co-writer Chris McKenna describes Strange as the voice of reason.
Jamie Foxx
Max Dillon
In "Spider-Man: No Way Home," Jamie Foxx reprises his role as an Oscorp electrical engineer who gained electric powers after an accident involving genetically modified electric eels. This character, originally appearing in "The Amazing Spider-Man 2" (2014), is redesigned in "No Way Home," shifting from his blue Ultimate Marvel-based design to a yellow one, resembling his mainstream comic book appearance.
Willem Dafoe
Norman Osborn
In "Spider-Man: No Way Home," Willem Dafoe returns as an alternate reality Oscorp CEO who becomes the Green Goblin after testing a strength enhancer. Digitally de-aged, his character features new tricks and an upgraded costume to better match his comic book look.
Alfred Molina
Otto Octavius
In "Spider-Man: No Way Home," Alfred Molina reprises his role as a scientist with four AI-controlled mechanical tentacles fused to his body from an accident. The film continues his story from "Spider-Man 2" (2004). Molina was digitally de-aged to match his earlier appearance, and the tentacles were created entirely with CGI.
Benedict Wong
Wong
In "Spider-Man: No Way Home," Benedict Wong reprises his role as Strange's mentor and friend who became the new Sorcerer Supreme during Strange's absence in the Blip.
Andrew Garfield
Peter Parker
In "Spider-Man: No Way Home," Andrew Garfield reprises his role as an alternate Peter Parker, known as "Peter-Three" and "The Amazing Spider-Man." Haunted by his failure to save Gwen Stacy, Garfield plays a tortured Parker who learns from past mistakes. He valued the opportunity to work with Tom Holland and Tobey Maguire and to tie up loose ends. In the climax, he redeems himself by saving MJ, echoing his failure with Stacy in "The Amazing Spider-Man 2."
Tobey Maguire
Peter Parker
In "Spider-Man: No Way Home," Tobey Maguire reprises his role from Raimi's trilogy as an alternate Peter Parker who uses organic webbing. Referred to as "Peter-Two" by other Spider-Men, Marvel's official website calls him the "Friendly Neighborhood Spider-Man." Maguire preferred minimal details about his character's life post-Spider-Man 3 (2007).
